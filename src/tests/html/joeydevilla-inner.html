    My “Trucker App” job interview homework assignment
    ios objective-c alamofire

    We were ninety minutes into a one-hour job interview when the CTO said We like your breadth of experience. Now wed like to test your depth. If we gave you a quick programming assignment, which would you prefer writing an iOS app, or an Android app? You should go with the one youre stronger in.

    Im a much stronger iOS programmer than an Android one. The smart move wouldve been to answer iOS, and be done with it.

    Instead, I replied Yes, but you want a guy who can lead a team of both iOS and Android developers. You really should give me both.

    The CTO raised an eyebrow, gave me a look, made a note on his pad, and said Hmmm...I think I will.

    This is either a total baller move (to borrow a turn of phrase the kids are using these day), or the end of that job prospect. But for the job that theyre trying to fill, taking on that challenge was the right thing to do.

    It had been a couple of years since Id touched C or Objective-C, but after bumping my head once or twice against C-isms (Oh yeah, pointers), I got into a groove and a few days later, I had an app that I could show them.

    I sent them my project files for the iOS app, along with this highlight reel of the app in action, which I made using Camtasia Studio. Tell me what you think while I work on the Android version.

    And thats the last I heard from them. Follow-up emails were met with silence. After the 6th email (If you have any questions or comments about the app, please feel free to contact me), it became apparent that this was a dead end, and it wouldnt be worth my while to continue working on the Android version. Perhaps Ill work on it as a way to sharpen my Kotlin skills.

    Feature or Apps Feb 2002
    Peekabooty
    c++ networking

    Peekabooty was an anti-censorship tool announced by the hacker and do-it-yourself media organization Cult of the Dead Cow at the DefCon conference in the summer of 2001. It was a peer-to-peer encrypted proxy system that circumvented internet censorship systems such as The Great Firewall of China, and has since been supplanted by VPN and similar anti-censorship technologies.

    Even though it was announced, the project wasnt actually implemented until Paul Baranowski and I worked on it starting December 2001, and presented it at the CodeCon conference in February 2002. Paul was the back-end developer, building the underlying networking software, while I was the front-end developer, and built and designed the user interface, which included the cute bear icons that the screen used to show other users on the network.

    Peekabooty got a considerable amount of press at the time. Heres a short list of articles: - The Register: Censor-buster Peek-A-Booty goes public - Neowin: Peek-A-Booty raises a few eyebrows at CodeCon - From the book Hacktivism and Cyberwars: Rebels with a Cause?: I am briefly mentioned on page 102 for my involvement with Peekabooty. - Spiegel Online: Vorhang auf fr Peek a Booty: Peekabooty, as covered by the online version of the German news magazine Der Spiegel.
